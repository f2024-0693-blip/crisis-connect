1 Abstract
Pakistan is among the most disaster-prone countries in the world. We present Crisis Connect, a multimodal deep learning framework that classifies disaster incidents from citizen-submitted photographs and text descriptions into four categories: Earthquake, Flood, Fire, and Traffic Incident. A ResNet-50 CNN (91.03%) and TF-IDF+MLP text classifier (89.47%) are combined via weighted fusion (70:30) achieving 95.07% test accuracy. The system is deployed as a publicly accessible web application.
Keywords: disaster classification, deep learning, multimodal fusion, ResNet-50, transfer learning, TF-IDF, emergency response, Pakistan
2 Introduction & Motivation

Pakistan ranks in the top ten most climate-vulnerable countries globally. The 2022 floods submerged one-third of national territory and displaced 33 million people. Disaster response remains manual and reactive.

Key limitations of existing systems:

  • Rely exclusively on image data — ignore text
  • Not trained on Pakistani disaster contexts
  • Not deployed in citizen-facing platforms

Our contributions:

  • Multimodal fusion — CNN + NLP weighted combination
  • Pakistan-relevant dataset — 2,537 curated images
  • Live deployment — publicly accessible web application
  • +3.14pp improvement over CNN-only baseline

4 Methodology

Problem formulation: Given image I and text T, predict class ŷ ∈ {Earthquake, Flood, Fire, Traffic Incident} and severity s ∈ {Low, Medium, High}.

ŷ = argmaxc [ α · PCNN(c|I) + (1−α) · PMLP(c|T) ]
Fusion equation — α = 0.70 (image weight)

CNN — ResNet-50 with transfer learning:

  • Pretrained on ImageNet (23M parameters frozen)
  • Custom head: Dropout(0.4) → Linear(2048→4)
  • Adam optimiser, lr=0.001, StepLR scheduler
  • Early stopping, patience=4, max 15 epochs
  • Augmentation: random crop, flip, rotation ±15°, colour jitter

NLP — TF-IDF + MLP:

  • TF-IDF: unigrams + bigrams, 3000 features, sublinear TF
  • MLP: 3000 → 512 → 256 → 4 (ReLU, L2 regularisation)
  • Adam optimiser, early stopping patience=10

7 Discussion & Limitations
  • Fusion improves performance because text disambiguates visually ambiguous scenes (e.g. flooded road vs waterlogged street)
  • Traffic Incident has lowest precision — visual overlap with fire and building damage scenes
  • Text corpus is synthetic — real citizen reports may differ; Urdu language not yet supported
  • Severity is rule-based — does not account for population density or infrastructure extent
  • Cold-start latency ~30s for model download on first request
8 Conclusion & Future Work

Crisis Connect demonstrates that multimodal fusion of image and text modalities consistently outperforms single-modality baselines for disaster classification. The 95.07% fusion accuracy represents a 3.14pp improvement over the CNN-only approach.

Future work:

  • Urdu language support via Whisper + deep-translator
  • Expand text corpus with real citizen reports
  • Integration with Crisis Connect mobile app backend
  • Data-driven severity estimation replacing rule-based approach
